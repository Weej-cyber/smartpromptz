Why I Built This

I was creating prompts but not getting the results I wanted. Frustrated, I asked the AI directly: "What do you actually need from me to give better outputs?" It gave me a systematic checklist - role, context, goals, constraints, examples.

That worked. I started getting much better results. But then I had a new problem: once I created a good prompt, I'd lose it. I'd have to scroll through endless chat history to find it, or worse, forget how I'd structured it and start over from scratch.

I realized I needed two things: guidance to create quality prompts consistently, and a way to save and organize the ones that worked. That's why I built ThinkPrompt - it gives you the systematic approach that actually works, then helps you build a personal library so you never lose a good prompt again.
